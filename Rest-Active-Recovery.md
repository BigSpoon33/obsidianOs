## Philosophy

Rest days are **essential** for muscle growth, injury prevention, and mental recovery. Active recovery helps maintain movement patterns while allowing your body to repair.

---

## Suggested Activities

### Option 1: Light Walking (20-30 min)
- Easy pace, conversational effort
- Outdoor if possible for mental health
- Focus on enjoying the movement
- **Benefit:** Blood flow aids recovery, low-impact cardio

### Option 2: Yoga Flow (20-30 min)
- Gentle vinyasa or restorative yoga
- Focus on mobility and breath
- Hold stretches for 30-60 seconds
- **Benefit:** Flexibility, mind-body connection

### Option 3: Foam Rolling Session (15-20 min)
- Target major muscle groups
- Spend extra time on sore areas
- Combine with light stretching
- **Benefit:** Myofascial release, reduces soreness

### Option 4: Swimming or Cycling (20-30 min)
- Very light intensity
- Focus on form and technique
- Should feel refreshing, not exhausting
- **Benefit:** Active recovery without impact

### Option 5: Meditation & Breathwork (10-20 min)
- Box breathing (4-4-4-4 pattern)
- Body scan meditation
- Gratitude practice
- **Benefit:** Nervous system recovery, stress reduction

## Full Body Stretching Routine

If you choose stretching, here's a simple routine:

1. **Cat-Cow Stretch** - 10 reps
2. **Child's Pose** - 60 seconds
3. **Downward Dog** - 30 seconds
4. **Hip Flexor Stretch** - 30 sec each side
5. **Hamstring Stretch** - 30 sec each side
6. **Quad Stretch** - 30 sec each side
7. **Shoulder Stretch** - 30 sec each side
8. **Spinal Twist** - 30 sec each side
9. **Chest Opener** - 60 seconds
10. **Savasana** - 3-5 minutes

## Recovery Checklist

On rest days, prioritize:
- [ ] 7-9 hours of quality sleep
- [ ] Adequate hydration (2-3L water)
- [ ] Nutritious meals with protein
- [ ] Light movement (not sedentary)
- [ ] Stress management
- [ ] Foam rolling or massage

---

## Listen to Your Body

**When to take FULL rest:**
- Feeling extremely fatigued
- Sore joints (not just muscles)
- Getting sick
- Poor sleep quality
- High stress levels

**When active recovery is good:**
- Mild muscle soreness (DOMS)
- Stiff but not in pain
- Good energy levels
- Feeling motivated to move

---

## Progress Tracking

Note in your daily journal:
- How you feel (energy, soreness)
- What recovery activity you did
- Sleep quality
- Readiness for tomorrow's workout
